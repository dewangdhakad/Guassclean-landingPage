§ FAQ

QUESTIONS,
ANSWERED.

Will it scratch my panels? +
No. The contact surfaces are microfiber, the brush is soft-bristle, and roller pressure is limited in firmware. The cleaning sequence is gentler than a manual squeegee crew on a deadline.
How does it move between rows? +
PR-1 traverses along a panel row autonomously. Row-to-row transfer is operator-assisted in the current version — one technician can manage an entire block. Fully autonomous transfer is on the roadmap.
What if it rains? Or a dust storm hits? +
The robot is IP65 sealed and parks itself on weather alerts from the cloud. Sessions resume automatically. Rajasthan weather is the design spec, not an edge case.
Why not waterless cleaning? +
Pure dry brushing smears cemented dust and risks micro-abrasion. 50ml of water per panel dissolves the bonded layer so microfiber can lift it clean — a deliberate middle path between water trucks and dry mops.
When can I get a pilot? +
We're onboarding pilot sites in Rajasthan now, prioritising O&M companies managing 10 MW or more. Email dewang@gaussclean.com — pilots are free to discuss, and early sites lock in launch pricing.
