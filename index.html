§ THE CLEANING PASS

FIVE STAGES.
ONE SWEEP.

Desert dust is silica — harder than the panel's coating. So PR-1 lifts it, never drags it. A dry brush sweeps loose grit into a sealed, gravity-fed bin before a single drop of water touches the glass. Watch one pass:

PR-1 · CLEANING HEAD · CROSS-SECTION ● DRY-FIRST
DUST IN · CLEAN OUT
01 · Sweep
Dry helical brush flicks loose dust up into a sealed shroud → hopper. No water, no vacuum.
02 · Damp
Microfiber with ~50 ml metered water dissolves the bonded residue dry brushing can't.
03 · Wipe
A soft squeegee lip clears the water line so nothing dries into spots.
04 · Collect
A helical roller walks the slurry to one low corner — captured, never dropped on panels below.
05 · Buff
A dry microfiber buff leaves the glass evaporative-dry and streak-free.

§ FAQ

QUESTIONS,
ANSWERED.

Will it scratch my panels? +
No — and that's the whole point of going dry-first. Desert dust is silica, harder than the anti-reflective coating, so wiping it wet would drag grit across the glass. PR-1 sweeps the loose dust off first, then uses soft microfiber and a metered mist only on what's left. Lift, don't drag.
Is it wet cleaning or dry cleaning? +
Both, in the right order. The first stage is fully dry — a brush lifts loose dust into a sealed bin with no water at all. Only the bonded layer that dry brushing can't shift gets ~50ml of water, then a dry buff finishes. You get dry-cleaning's water savings with wet-cleaning's spotless finish.
How does it capture dust without a vacuum? +
The brush works like a sealed street-sweeper, not a vacuum. Its housing is closed against the glass, so dust the brush lifts is carried inside the shroud to a low corner and drops by gravity into a sealed hopper. No blower means power falls from roughly 300W to 50–80W — smaller battery, longer runtime, near-silent operation.
How does it move between rows? +
PR-1 traverses along a panel row autonomously. Row-to-row transfer is operator-assisted in the current version — one technician can manage an entire block. Fully autonomous transfer is on the roadmap.
What if it rains? Or a dust storm hits? +
The robot is IP65 sealed and parks itself on weather alerts from the cloud. Sessions resume automatically. Rajasthan weather is the design spec, not an edge case.
